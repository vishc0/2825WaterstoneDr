# Salability Assessment and Action Plan

Property: 2825 Waterstone Drive, Cumming, GA 30041  
Assessment date: July 12, 2026

## Verdict

**Salability: Good, but price-sensitive. Overall assessment: approximately 7/10.**

The home has enough space, updates, photography quality, school/location appeal, and genuine differentiators to attract buyers around $1.1M–$1.15M. It is not a difficult property to market. The challenge is converting interest into an offer while buyers can choose newer, golf, gated, pool, acreage, covered-outdoor-living, and same-street alternatives at similar prices.

The current $1.15M ask is reasonable as a market test. The most probable clean sale appears closer to **$1.10M–$1.14M**, subject to showing feedback, condition, contract terms, and documentation.

## Why buyers will engage

- Very large home at only about $180 per total finished sq ft.
- Updated, bright presentation rather than an untouched 2013 builder interior.
- Full second kitchen and bedroom/bath arrangement create credible in-law or extended-family use.
- Creek sound, wooded outlook, double decks and sunset orientation create a memorable emotional hook.
- Private office and wired-networking potential fit executive and hybrid-work buyers.
- Main-level guest bedroom and bath broaden accessibility and family use.
- Media, recreation and fitness rooms convert basement area into usable lifestyle value.
- South Forsyth schools and access to GA-400 support relocation demand.

## Why buyers may hesitate

- At $1.15M, alternatives offer golf views, gated entry, covered porches, pools, 3- or 4-car garages, larger lots, or newer systems.
- The realtor's current listing and major portals present the garage as two-car; public marketing should use that same fact.
- Public records and MLS disagree on square footage and bedroom/bath count.
- Basement permit/final status is not obvious from syndicated public records.
- Roof and HVAC age can create fear of near-term capital expense.
- Appraisal is $50K below list.
- Same-street 2815 Waterstone is listed $50K lower.
- General 30041 pricing is down year over year, only about 11% of homes sell above list, and more than one-third show price reductions.

## Expected market path

[The current 30041 market](https://www.redfin.com/zipcode/30041/housing-market) averages roughly 43–46 days, with hot homes around 18 days. The subject has approximately nine active days as of July 12, but longer cumulative coming-soon exposure.

- Approximate outcome: $1.05M–$1.10M, potentially with concessions, unless price or risk package is materially improved.

## Pricing decision rules

Do not react to views alone. Use qualified-showing conversion.

### Hold $1.15M temporarily when

- Active days are still under approximately 18–21.
- Qualified showings are occurring.
- Feedback is positive on price or says the home is a finalist.
- Second showings or due-diligence requests are occurring.
- 2815 Waterstone remains active without a decisive advantage.

### Reassess price when

- There have been roughly 8–12 qualified showings without a second showing or offer.
- Two or more independent agents give the same price-to-condition feedback.
- 2815 Waterstone or 2001/1068 Windermere goes pending while subject traffic slows.
- The listing passes approximately 21 active days without credible buyer progression.

### If reducing

- **Measured strategy:** approximately $1,125,000. This aligns more closely with the Redfin estimate and market sale-to-list behavior while maintaining a premium over 2815 Waterstone.
- **Decisive strategy:** approximately $1,099,000. This crosses the $1.1M search threshold and could materially expand the buyer pool.
- Avoid a small $5K–$10K reduction that does not change search exposure or buyer perception.

## Negotiation preparation

At a $1.15M ask, buyers may attempt to use the $1.10M appraisal, original systems, and public-record differences to justify a lower offer. Prepare responses in advance:

- Price against the direct $1.205M Waterstone sale, June $1.14M Windermere sale, and pending $1.15M–$1.175M functional comps.
- Quantify the full second kitchen, basement finish, decks, landscaping and recent renovations.
- Separate cosmetic objections from health/safety or end-of-life system risk.
- Compare net offers, not headline price: closing costs, rate buydown, repair cap, appraisal gap, financing, contingency length, and closing date.
- A $1.14M offer with minimal concessions can be better than a $1.16M offer with a large buydown and repair exposure.

## Final assessment

The home is **sellable at or near the current pricing band**, but the evidence does not support treating $1.15M as guaranteed. Its marketing strengths are real and early portal engagement is encouraging. The fastest path to a strong contract is to eliminate data/document risk, defend the value premium over 2815 Waterstone, monitor the new Windermere competition, and make a disciplined decision at the 18–21 active-day checkpoint.
