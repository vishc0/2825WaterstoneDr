## Engagement snapshots

Engagement numbers are volatile and crawl-dependent, so they should not be treated as seller-dashboard totals.

- A recent Zillow search snapshot showed about **1,129 views and 43 saves after seven active days** (approximately 3.8% save-to-view).
- An earlier cached Zillow page showed **277 views and 9 saves after six coming-soon days**.
- A Redfin crawl showed **169 views and 5 favorites**, a “top 10% of views” message, and 115 photos.
- These signals indicate strong awareness. They do not prove showing quality or offer intent.

The correct conversion questions are: qualified showings, second showings, buyer-agent follow-ups, disclosure/inspection-document requests, and written offers.

## Material discrepancies and correction priorities

### Priority 1 — correct immediately

1. **Garage count** — The realtor's current listing and major portals show two garage spaces. Use the realtor's listing as the public source of truth and remove the former three-car/two-door wording from owner marketing and change requests.
2. **Flooring** — Structured fields do not clearly communicate the full replacement scope: LVP on first/main and terrace levels, replacement carpet in all bedrooms, and redone stairs.
3. **Cabinet finish** — Structured fields show stained cabinetry even though the remarks and photographs describe renovated light/white cabinetry.
4. **HOA amount** — $1,200/year is supported, but Zillow/Redfin payment displays can show $0.
5. **Status and active date** — Ensure both MLS records are active and synchronized, with the correct on-market date and no unintended duplicate/coming-soon record.
6. **View classification** — “Mountain(s)” and “water view” appear in structured data while “waterfront: none” also appears. Use precise language such as wooded creek setting, seasonal creek view, and year-round creek sound if verified. Avoid a mountain-view claim unless photographically defensible.

### Priority 2 — document before buyer due diligence

1. **Public record versus MLS area** — Redfin public record shows 5,977 sq ft, while MLS shows 6,377 sq ft from owner data. The 400-sq-ft difference should be explained by appraisal, measurements, floor plan, or permit records.
2. **Public record versus MLS bed/bath count** — Public record shows 5/4; MLS shows 6/5; owner describes a possible 7-bedroom configuration. This invites permit, egress and appraisal questions and should be resolved before changing official fields.
3. **Permit history** — The county's 2013 construction report shows permit 20130051, 3,758 finished sq ft and 1,307 unfinished sq ft. Portal permit history surfaces only the original construction permit. Locate any later basement, deck, bedroom/bath, or renovation permits and final inspections.
4. **Living-area source** — Realtor.com labels the 6,377 sq ft as owner-sourced. Add appraisal measurements or a professional floor plan to increase confidence.
5. **Upgrade claim** — Support the “over $100,000” statement with an itemized upgrade sheet, approximate dates, contractors, permits, warranties, and receipts where available.

### Priority 3 — improve buyer search and differentiation

Add verified features to structured MLS fields and the buyer-agent packet, not only the prose:

- Attached two-car garage, consistent with the realtor's current listing.
- Full second kitchen and in-law layout.
- Media room, game/recreation area, and fitness room.
- Wired Ethernet/networking infrastructure.
- Ecobee thermostats and transferable smart-home features.
- Tankless water heater.
- Plantation shutters.
- Rebuilt double decks and sunset orientation.
- Year-round creek sound and seasonal view, with accurate non-waterfront wording.
- Epoxy garage floor.
- Custom primary closet.
- Exterior-vented range hood.

## Listing presentation assessment

### What is working

- Strong twilight hero image and professional photography.
- Clear executive/multi-generational lifestyle story.
- Large finished basement is shown as useful living space, not generic square footage.
- Recent renovations are visible and support move-in-ready positioning.
- Creek, decks, sunset, office, and second kitchen distinguish the home from standard six-bedroom listings.
- At $180 per total sq ft, the listing appears inexpensive relative to the selected active-comp median near $201 per sq ft.

### What may be reducing conversion

- 113–115 syndicated photos can dilute the strongest images if there are duplicates or weak angles. The first 12–15 images should tell the complete value story.
- Garage and other structured-data errors may remove the property from saved searches.
- Public-record differences may cause buyer agents to pause until documentation is supplied.
- “Mountain view” and similar overbroad fields can undermine trust if the on-site experience does not match.
- The listing competes directly against a same-street $1.10M home and several $1.125M–$1.20M homes with golf, gated, pool, acreage, newer-system, or covered-outdoor-living advantages.

Ask the listing agent to provide screenshots or a PDF of both FMLS 7791792 and GAMLS 10789950 and confirm:

Do this before considering a price change. Better data can increase qualified search exposure without sacrificing price.
