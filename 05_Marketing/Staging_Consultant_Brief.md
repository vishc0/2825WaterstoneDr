# Staging Consultant Brief

Property: 2825 Waterstone Drive, Cumming, Georgia

Purpose: Prepare the home to present as a premium South Forsyth executive residence with strong lifestyle, multi-generational, and relocation-buyer appeal.

## Positioning Concept

This home should not feel like a standard suburban listing. It should read as a polished, move-in-ready executive home with meaningful lifestyle advantages:

- A quiet creek-lot setting with year-round water sounds
- Indoor-outdoor living through double rear decks
- A finished basement with full in-law suite, kitchen, media room, and game room
- A strong work-from-home and technology profile
- A South Forsyth location suited for executive relocation, healthcare, technology, and multi-generational buyers

The staging goal is to make buyers feel three things quickly:

1. This home is elevated and well cared for.
2. The layout solves real family and hosting needs.
3. The basement and outdoor setting create value beyond ordinary square footage.

## Target Buyer Read

Staging should be designed for buyers who are likely comparing this home against newer or similarly priced executive homes in South Forsyth, Alpharetta, Johns Creek, and nearby Forsyth County neighborhoods.

Priority personas:

- Executive relocation buyer
- Technology professional or remote/hybrid executive
- Healthcare executive
- Entrepreneur
- Multi-generational family
- High-income Indian family
- Corporate transfer family
- Buyer moving from California, New York, Illinois, New Jersey, or Washington

The home should feel aspirational but not fragile. Avoid overly formal, museum-like styling. The best direction is warm modern luxury: clean, bright, layered, family-friendly, and photographable.

## Design Direction

Recommended style:

- Warm transitional luxury
- Light neutral palette anchored by Alabaster walls, white trim, and updated flooring
- Sophisticated contrast through black, charcoal, natural wood, brass, stone, and greenery
- Minimal but intentional accessories
- Larger-scale art where rooms need stronger photography impact
- Elevated bedding, towels, coffee-table styling, office styling, and outdoor living cues

Avoid:

- Farmhouse cliches
- Heavy traditional furniture
- Overly gray interiors
- Busy rugs or small-scale art
- Strong personal, religious, political, or niche cultural styling
- Too many small decorative objects
- Furniture layouts that make rooms photograph smaller

## Priority Areas

### 1. Entry and Two-Story Foyer

Objective: Immediate luxury arrival.

Staging needs:

- Clean sightline from front door into the home
- One strong focal piece if space allows: console, large mirror, art, or greenery
- No clutter, shoes, small baskets, or transitional family storage
- Lighting checked and fully warm-white

Buyer reaction target: "This feels high-end and cared for."

### 2. Main Living Area and Fireplace

Objective: Sell comfort, scale, and gathering.

Staging needs:

- Furniture arranged to emphasize openness and conversation
- Fireplace treated as a primary focal point
- Soft seating, quality pillows, throw, large rug if needed
- Keep traffic flow obvious from kitchen to living to deck
- Use greenery or natural texture to connect visually to creek setting

Buyer reaction target: "This is where our family would spend time."

### 3. Kitchen and Dining

Objective: Reinforce recent upgrades and entertaining value.

Staging needs:

- Counters mostly clear
- One or two premium lifestyle cues: fruit bowl, cookbook stand, florals, coffee station, or serving board
- Dining table staged for scale, not clutter
- Cabinet refinishing and backsplash should remain visually prominent
- Remove magnets, personal notes, dish racks, excess appliances, and visible cleaning supplies

Buyer reaction target: "The kitchen feels updated, bright, and easy to live in."

### 4. Office With Glass Doors

Objective: Capture executive and technology buyer demand.

Staging needs:

- Stage as a serious executive office, not a spare room
- Clean desk, task lamp, simple art, one plant
- Hide cords aggressively
- Include subtle tech readiness without showing equipment clutter
- Make glass doors a selling point in photos

Buyer reaction target: "I can work from here immediately."

### 5. Primary Suite

Objective: Create emotional attachment.

Staging needs:

- Hotel-level bedding with strong pillows and layered textiles
- Nightstands balanced with lamps
- Seating moment if space allows
- Bath counters styled with spa cues: towels, tray, greenery, minimal premium bottles
- Closets edited heavily

Buyer reaction target: "This feels like a retreat."

### 6. Basement, In-Law Suite, Media Room, and Game Room

Objective: Convert basement square footage into perceived premium value.

Staging needs:

- Make the in-law suite feel complete and dignified, not secondary
- Kitchenette/full kitchen should be staged simply and cleanly
- Media room should clearly read as movie/sports/family entertainment
- Game room should feel flexible: teen lounge, gym alternative, play/game zone, or hosting area
- Avoid storage spillover anywhere buyers will walk
- Use lighting to prevent basement spaces from feeling dark

Buyer reaction target: "This gives us options other homes do not."

### 7. Decks and Creek-Lot Lifestyle

Objective: Turn the outdoor setting into a memory.

Staging needs:

- Stage at least one deck as an outdoor living room
- Stage another as coffee, dining, or quiet retreat if furniture allows
- Use clean cushions, planters, lanterns, outdoor rug if appropriate
- Clear leaves, debris, cobwebs, tools, hoses, and visible maintenance items
- Maximize buyer awareness of creek sound and seasonal views

Buyer reaction target: "This setting feels peaceful and special."

## Photographer Coordination

Staging should be completed before photography and should support these photo priorities:

- Strong front exterior
- Two-story foyer arrival
- Open kitchen/living connection
- Fireplace/living room focal shot
- Office with glass doors
- Primary suite and spa bath
- Basement in-law suite
- Media and game areas
- Double decks
- Creek-lot context
- Twilight exterior if available

## Fast Timeline Priorities

If staging availability is limited, prioritize in this order:

1. Consultation and walkthrough
2. Edit, declutter, furniture placement, and styling plan
3. Living room, kitchen, primary suite, office
4. Basement in-law suite and media/game areas
5. Decks and outdoor lifestyle styling
6. Photo-day final fluffing

## Questions for the Stager

- Can you support a fast listing-prep timeline?
- Do you offer occupied-home staging using existing furniture plus accessories?
- Can you bring luxury-scale art, bedding, pillows, greenery, and outdoor accents?
- Do you have experience with executive homes in Cumming, South Forsyth, Alpharetta, Johns Creek, or North Atlanta?
- Can you stage or consult specifically for photography impact?
- Can you return for photo-day touch-up if needed?
- What are your consultation, partial staging, and accessory-rental costs?
- What rooms would you prioritize if we only have time for the highest-ROI work?

## Success Criteria

The final staged home should help justify premium positioning by making the property feel:

- Move-in ready
- Bright and current
- Executive-level
- Family-functional
- Multi-generational capable
- Peaceful and nature-connected
- Photographically strong
- Worth lingering in during showings
