## For 2825 Waterstone

Given the appraisal at **$1.1M**, the goal is no longer to justify $1.1M. The goal is to create enough buyer emotion and differentiation to push:

* $1.15M = +$50k
* $1.20M = +$100k
* $1.25M = +$150k

The master bath photos you posted are already above average for Forsyth County. I would not spend $30k–$50k tearing it apart.

My estimate:

### High ROI

* New luxury mirrors with integrated LED lighting
* Modern matte black or brushed brass hardware
* Replace all toilets with contemporary skirted one-piece models
* Professional grout cleaning/color sealing
* Luxury staging accessories
* Premium towels, tray, spa decor
* Better lighting and photography

Cost:

* $2,500–$7,500

Potential buyer perception gain:

* Similar to a $15k–$25k renovation

### Low ROI

* Retile shower
* Replace tub
* Replace vanities
* Rebuild shower

Cost:

* $25k–$60k

Likely return before sale:

* Less than cost

For a home about to hit the market, I'd invest in presentation and staging, not major bathroom reconstruction. The bath already looks like a builder-upgraded executive home; the biggest value increase now comes from creating a luxury model-home feel rather than replacing functional finishes.
