## 3. Main Topics Covered

### Topic A: U.S. Housing Market Overview

Key points discussed:

* National housing market is slowing rather than booming.
* Mortgage rates remain elevated.
* Inventory has increased compared to pandemic years.
* Affordability remains a major challenge.
* Luxury homes are performing better than entry-level housing in many markets.

Conclusion:

* Market is transitioning toward a buyer-friendlier environment.

---

### Topic B: Possibility of a Housing Market Crash

Question:

* Will the U.S. housing market crash in 2026?

Summary of discussion:

Reasons a major crash is considered unlikely:

* Homeowners have significant equity.
* Lending standards are stronger than pre-2008.
* Many homeowners have low fixed-rate mortgages.
* No large national inventory oversupply.

Expected outcome:

* Flat to slightly declining prices nationally.
* Some regional corrections.
* Certain markets may experience 5–10% declines.
* No expectation of a 2008-style collapse.

---

### Topic C: Cumming, GA Housing Market

Market conditions discussed:

#### Approximate Market Metrics

* Typical home values around $600K–$605K.
* Price decline roughly:

  * Zillow: ~2.5% YoY
  * Redfin: ~4.8% YoY
* Homes taking approximately:

  * 36–67 days to sell
* Inventory increased significantly.
* Many homes selling below asking price.

#### Buyer Environment

* More negotiating power.
* More inventory available.
* Less bidding-war activity.

#### Seller Environment

* Well-priced homes still sell.
* Overpriced homes sit on the market longer.
* Pricing accuracy is critical.

#### Local Strengths

* Strong schools.
* Population growth.
* South Forsyth desirability.
* Executive buyer demand.

Conclusion:

* Cumming remains healthier than many U.S. markets but is no longer a strong seller's market.
